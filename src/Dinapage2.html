What is a Black Hole?

A Black Hole is basically a vacuum in space, but not just any vacuum, a super duper strong one. It is a region of spacetime where the gravity is extremely strong to the point that nothing (literally nothing) can escape from it, not even light! It is not like a planet or star where there is a surface, rather this region could be described as one where matter collapsed on itself. Simply put, large mass in a tiny area equals insanely strong gravity.

How are black holes formed?

In simple terms, when a star dies a black hole forms. Sounds like a myth from a dark fairytale, but no that’s real. Most black holes form from what remains of a dead large star in something called a supernova explosion. When the center of an extremely massive star collapses in upon itself, a Stellar black hole is formed. So basically a black hole is a zombie star.

What would happen if something were to go in a black hole?

Have you ever wondered what would happen if let's say a human or a cat or even earth would go in a black hole? There are a lot of guesses as to what lies behind a black hole, some say it’s a portal to a parallel universe, some say it’s to heaven (well, either way you’d probably be dead, so the second has more likeliness). Anyway, physics on the other hand says this: “A black hole would stretch an astronaut's body into a thin ribbon”. In other words you’re also dead according to physics. So the lesson here is: Don’t go in a black hole. The cat would probably have same doom fall upon it, and earth would cease to exist anymore.
