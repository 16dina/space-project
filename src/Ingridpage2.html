Industry

As technology progresses humans have found various ways to industrialize space. That's right, not even space can escape capitalism. The global space industry generates a revenue of $350 billion dollars annually and there are estimations that by 2040 this will increase to more than $1 trillion, if not more.

Below you can find several topics within the space industry.

Satellites

Satellites are responsible for making many of our day-to-day communication needs possible.

Tourism

Space tourism is a new industry that so far has been exclusive to the 1% of the population.

SpaceX

SpaceX is a private company that designs, builds, and launches spacecrafts.

Meteor Mining

Meteor mining is a hypothetical industry believed to have great future potential.

Space Junk

There is over 200.000 pieces of space junk in the earths low orbit threatening to make the low atmosphere unusable.

Satellites

A satellite is an object that orbits around a star or a planet. As an example, the moon is a satellite that orbits around the earth. However, when we speak about satellites we often mean satellites of a mechanical nature. Humans have launched more than 6,500 satellites into space! Half of these satellites are inactive, but the rest responsible for providing several services used in our day-to-day lives.

Communication satellites are the most common type of artificial satellite. They provide services for television, telephone service, radio, internet, and also military applications. There are currently 2,224 communication satellites orbiting Earth connecting the world together.

The worlds entire GPS(Global Positioning System) system is created by 27 satellites. They are responsible for tracing your location while using services such as Google Maps. Without them, we would all be stuck using plain old paper maps while on the road.

Tourism

Space tourism might sound like a futuristic farfetched idea, but it has actually been around since the early 2000s. Russia sold the free seats on their spacecrafts headed for the international space station for US$20-25 million per trip. Tourism was believed to be one of the earliest markets to emerge from the growth of the space industry. However, as the size of the crew headed for the international space station increased, Russia could no longer sell the available seats and the tourism halted. In 2019 NASA announced that private astronauts would be allowed to travel to the international space station. The price for this is US$35.000 per day at the station, with the cost of the trip there and back being at US$50 million. It is clear that for now commercial space travel is reserved for the top 1% in the world, but maybe some day the opportunity will become more accessible.

SpaceX

SpaceX is an American commercial space company funded by Elon Musk. The goal of the company is to reduce the cost of space transportation and eventually colonize Mars. The company produces heavy launch vehicles, rocket engines, and communication satellites. Currently, the company is developing a satellite constellation system named Starlink in order to provide commercial internet access. As of May 2022 the constellation has over 2400 small satellites within the system. SpaceX is also one of the companies that offer paid commercial spaceflights. It is the first private company to launch, orbit, and recover a spacecraft and focuses on developing reusable spacecrafts.

Asteroid Mining

Asteroids Mining is an industry believed to have great promise in the future. Many asteroids contain large amounts of metal, some which are rare and valuable. Limitations in technology as well as the high cost of space travel prevents the industry from being viable. However, the industry is not abandoned, the players within the field have instead set their sights on long-term, distant goals. Several asteroids have been identified and analyzed proving they contain metals such as nickel, gold, and platinum. An S-type asteroid of a small size contains around 650.000kg of metal. The larger M-type asteroid contains 10 times more metal than the S-type.

Space Junk

Space junk may seem like a silly problem, but it has caused several issues for the space industry. Due to the speed the earth orbits at, if a spacecraft makes contact with space junk it can cause a significant amount of damage. In fact damage through space junk has caused projects to be abandoned, creating more space junk in an endless circle. Collisions could start chain reactions taking down more satellites. It can take centuries before the junk returns to earth.

Measurers are being taken to prevent the creating of more space junk, but not much can be currently done to remove the junk from earths orbit.
