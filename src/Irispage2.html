Humans in space

Astronaut

We call people who travel into space an astronaut. Normally this term is only for professional space travellers who are trained, and followed a spaceflight program. Sometimes it’s also applied to anyone who goes to space, even including tourists. The term is for everyone regardless of nationality, but astronauts fielded by Russia are known as cosmonauts.

First space travelers

600 astronauts have flown in space since 1961 and 12 people walked on the Moon. The first man to travel to space was Soviet Yuri Gagarin, he launched on 12 April 1961. He spent 108 minutes in space and he orbited around the Earth. On June 16th 1963 the first woman in space was launched. It was Soviet Valentina Tereshkova. She orbited the Earth for almost 3 days.

Apollo 11 was the spaceflight that firstly landed people on the Moon. Neil Armstrong was the first man to ever walk on the Moon. This happened on July 20, 1969, together with Buzz Aldrin. Neil Armstrong said the famous sentence: “That’s one small step for a man. One giant leap for mankind.”

Health and nutrition

There are a lot of health risks associated with going to space. Think of loss of bone and muscle, loss of eyesight, barotrauma… After a study supported by NASA in 2012, it showed that going to space may harm the brain. In 2020 after another study it became clear that there is evidence that the brain can change through growth and reorganization...

The nutrition is different on Earth as it is in space. They have space-nutrition specialists to ensure that they have enough nutrients. This can change over time, due to many physiological changes. Think of the weightlessness, this has a huge effect on the body. The astronauts can choose between different things to eat and drink: fruits, chicken, seafood, nuts, coffee, tea, lemonade… The food is packed in disposable packages, so they don’t fly away. The drinks are in a bag, which they drink with a straw.

Astronauts don’t shower in space, they use liquid soap, water and rinse less shampoo. These are packed in pouches. They sleep in small sleeping compartments and in a sleeping bag. They strap their body’s so that they don’t float around while they are sleeping.

Milestones

  • The youngest person to travel to space is Oliver Daemen (The Netherlands) , he was 18 years and 11 months old. This happened on July 20, 2021.
  • The longest time spent in space is 438 days. Valeri Polyakov did this in 1994.
  • The most spaceflights someone did was 7, this was done by Jerry L. Ross and also Franklin Chang-Diaz.
