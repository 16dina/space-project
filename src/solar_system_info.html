Our Solar System

The Solar System is a system of the Sun and planets that go around the Sun. There are 8 planets: 4 gas and ice giants (the largest planets), and 4 terrestrial planets. A terrestrial planet is a planet with a rocky body. There are also an unknown amount of dwarf planets and small Solar System bodies. The Solar System was formed 4.6 billion years ago caused by the Big Bang. It is part of a galaxy known as the Milky Way. The biggest star is the Sun.

The Sun

The Sun is in the center of the Solar System, it’s a ball of hot plasma. All the planets turn around the Sun, on Earth this takes a year. Because of the sun, life on Earth is possible. The Sun is 80% bigger than the other stars and it has a diameter of 1.39 million kilometres (that’s 109 times the diameter of the Earth). It’s surface temperature is approximately 5 505 degrees Celsius. The light of the Sun takes 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach the Earth.

Mercury

The planet Mercury is the smallest planet and it is of the type terrestrial. This planet is the closest to the Sun. Mercury doesn’t have an atmosphere because it’s too small, that’s why during the day the temperature can be 427 degrees Celsius, but during the night -173°C. It also doesn’t have any moons and takes 88 (Earth) days to travel around the Sun.

Venus

If you look at the order of the planets, you can see that Venus is the second planet from the Sun. This planet is also of the type terrestrial and also doesn’t have any moons. A year on Venus takes 225 days and it shines the brightest out of all the planets. Although Venus isn’t the closest to the Sun, it has the hottest surface, the maximum temperature is 482°C and the minimum is 438°C.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun, it is also a terrestrial type planet and has the nickname “blue planet”. This is because the Earth’s surface is made up for about 70% of water (ocean, lakes, rivers, polar ice…). The other 30% is land (continents, islands…). This is for so far we know the only planet with living creatures. Earth has one moon, you can find more info on the moon page. It takes the Earth one year and 6 hours to orbit around the Sun. That’s why we have once in four years a leap year. The mean temperature is 14°C.

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun, the type of this planet is also terrestrial. It’s often called “red planet”, this is because the planet has a lot of iron that started to rust. Mars has a thin atmosphere, that’s why the temperature differs a lot. The maximum temperature is 20 degrees Celsius and the minimum is -140°C. It also has polar ice just like the Earth. This planet takes 687 days to go around the Sun. Mars has 2 moons, these are very small.

Jupiter is the 5th planet from the Sun, it’s a gas giant. It’s the largest planet in our Solar System. As far as we know, Jupiter has 16 moons. It takes Jupiter 12 years to orbit around the sun. The atmosphere of this planet is made up of poisonous deadly gases. Jupiter has also rings, but these aren’t as visible as the ones from Saturn. The mean temperature of Jupiter is -110°C.

Saturn is the 6th planet from the Sun, it’s also a gas giant just like Jupiter. This is the second largest planet and is also known as the ringed planet. To orbit the Sun, it takes 29.5 years. The rings are made of a huge amount of sand and stones but also ice particles, that turn around the planet. Saturn has 82 moons and the mean temperature is -140°C.

Uranus is the 7th planet from the Sun and this planet is an ice giant. This planet also has rings, 13 that we know of. It has 27 moons and it takes 84 years to turn around the Sun. The mean temperature of this planet is -95°C. We don’t know as much about this planet, because it is so far away from Earth.

Neptune is the 8th planet from the Sun and also the farthest-known planet. It is an ice giant and it has 5 rings (that we know of). This planet has 14 moons and looks a lot like the planet Uranus. They are both blue and the atmospheres looks a lot like the one from Uranus. It takes 164.8 years to orbit around the Sun. The mean temperature of Neptune is -200°C.

Dwarf planets

A dwarf planet is a small planetary-mass object. It has its own orbit around the Sun. Since 2006 there are guidelines about what a planet is, there are 3 conditions and Pluto didn’t meet the last condition. That’s why Pluto is the most well-known dwarf planet, it was first considered as a planet. Other dwarf planets are Ceres, Eris, Haumea, Makemake, Gonggong… Dwarf planets also have moons, Pluto has 5 that we know of.
