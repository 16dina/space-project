The MOON

Introduction

The moon is perhaps one of the most intriguing things for humans when it comes to exploring space. It’s a given since it is fairly closer to us than other objects like planets and stars. From thinking the moon is made of cheese as kids, to fairy tales about the man on the moon, to thinking the moon is following us. Many thoughts came to mind when looking at the moon, but not all of them are true. So what is the moon exactly?


What is the moon? and where is it?

The moon is a natural satellite, not a star nor a planet. It is actually the fifth-largest moon in the solar system. It is Earth’s only natural satellite, and it orbits around it within 27 days. The moon was formed 4.5 billion years ago because of a collision between the earth and another planet. Some scientists even think that the moon broke off from Earth after the collision. The moon is about 385,000 km away from Earth. That is about 30 Earths away. It orbits counterclockwise around the Earth.



What is the moon made of?

The moon’s mass = 7.35 x 1022 kg which is about 1.2% of Earth’s mass. In other words, Earth weighs 81 times more than the moon. (Earth is fatter lol). It consists of the following material (Sadly doesn’t include cheese):

  1. 43% oxygen
  2. 20% silicon
  3. 19% magnesium
  4. 10% iron
  5. 3% calcium
  6. 3% aluminum
  7. 0.42% chromium
  8. 0.18% titanium
  9. 0.12% manganese

Importance of the moon

Imagine a wobbly earth, that would be the reality if there was no moon. The moon keeps the earth from wobbling violently while it’s spinning. This helps keep the climate in check . It controls the length of the day and the ocean tides. All this affects the biological cycles of lifeforms on our planet.

Is the moon cool?

The moon is fairly popular and can be considered a cool kid. Here are some cool facts about the moon:

The 5 facts switch every 10 seconds:

  1. The moon is drifting away from the earth! It is moving around 3.8 cm away from Earth every year.

Fact #1:

The moon is drifting away from the earth! It is moving around 3.8 cm away from Earth every year.

Fact #2:

The moon has gravity. It is about 1/6 of the Earth’s gravity, which means that you would weigh mush less if you stand on the moon.

Fact #3:

The moon has impacts on marine animals. From changes in feeding behavior when there is change in the ocean tides, to mass spawning specifically at the time of a full moon every year.

Fact #4:

Ever heard of Earthquakes? Well, guess what? There is a “Moonquake” too! Although it is much weaker than Earthquakes, it last longer (for up to half an hour).

Fact #5:

There is water on the moon! In 2018, NASA confirmed that water ice is present on the surface at the Moon. Based on remote observations by radar instruments, the moon has over 600 billion kilograms of water ice. That's enough to fill at least 240,000 Olympic-sized swimming pools. And according to assistant researcher Shua Li at the University of Hawaii's Institute of Geophysics and Planetology, we can actually drink that water!
